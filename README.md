## Why Toenail Fungus Is So Stubborn (And Why Sprays Are Different)

Toenail fungus — medically called onychomycosis — is one of the most common and genuinely annoying conditions around. According to WebMD, it affects millions of adults and doesn't go away on its own. Left alone, it spreads to adjacent nails, thickens the nail plate, and eventually gets painful when you walk.

The problem with most OTC creams? They sit on the surface. Toenails are dense. Fungus lives *under* the nail plate, in the nail bed where creams can't easily reach.

Prescription oral antifungals (like terbinafine) do reach it — but they come with liver-function monitoring, potential drug interactions, and a months-long treatment course. Not everyone wants to go that route for what amounts to a cosmetic annoyance.

Mist spray technology takes a different approach. By releasing the formula in fine particles at higher velocity, products like ProNail Complex aim to get the active ingredients beneath the nail plate without requiring you to file down the nail first. Whether that works as advertised depends heavily on the formulation — which is why ingredient quality matters.

## How to Use ProNail Complex (Step-by-Step)

Getting the application right makes a real difference in results. Here's the process:

1. **Shower or wash your feet first.** Warm water softens the nail and opens pores, which helps the formula absorb deeper. Pat dry completely.
2. **Hold the bottle 6–8 inches from the affected nail.** Don't press the bottle against the nail — the mist needs to land in a fine layer.
3. **Spray an even coat across the nail and surrounding skin.** Cover the cuticle and the nail edge where fungus tends to hide.
4. **Let it absorb for 30–60 seconds.** The formula is fast-drying, so you're not waiting long.
5. **Put on clean cotton socks.** This keeps the treated area clean and — according to the brand — creates an environment that's inhospitable to fungus while the formula continues working.
6. **Repeat twice daily.** Once after your morning shower, once before bed.

Most users notice the first visible changes around weeks 3–4 — usually a fading of yellow discoloration. Significant nail regrowth in a cleaner state typically shows around weeks 8–12. Full recovery can take 3–6 months depending on how advanced the infection is and how fast your nails grow.
